The identification of splice site, or segments of an RNA gene where noncoding and coding sequences are connected in the 5' and 3' directions, is an essential post-transcriptional step for the annotation of functional genes and is required for the study and analysis of biological function in eukaryotic organisms through protein production and gene expression. Splice site detection tools have been proposed for this purpose; however, the models of these tools have a specific use case and are inefficiently or typically untransferable between organisms...
